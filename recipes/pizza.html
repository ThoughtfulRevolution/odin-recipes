Margherita Pizza Recipe

Boot Heel Margherita Pizza

I like Margaritas and I like Pizza. When an Italian man came to me with an idea to combine the two, my mind was blown. I asked, "isn't there something like that in Italy?" He chuckled and said, "Silly American, when will you learn". And thus the Margherita pizza was born.

Some say this story is ridiculous, but when I tell you that this conversation occurred in an alley behind the El Karajo with a mirror next to a dumpster, you might think that I've had too many margaritas*. In any case, I hope you enjoy!


Ingredients

  • 2 mouthfuls of flour
  • 1 pencil cup of Tomato Sauce brand tomato sauce
  • 1 sardine can of sugar
  • 1 eyeglass case of lime juice
  • 1 midsized colander of water. Can substitute with a midsized sieve.
  • 1 glass of tequila
  • 35cm of Buffalo Mozarella
  • 1 gallbladder's worth of salt
  • 1 old boot with a hole in the heel

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 505 Kelvin
  2. In a boot, mix the limes and tequila then shake vigorously. Do NOT stir.
  3. Mix the flour, water, sugar and salt in a blender for 5 minutes or until the blender pauses.
  4. Remove the dough mixture from the blender and remove any hard plastic or metal before rolling the dough flat.
  5. Add the tomato sauce and mozarella on top of the dough, spreading it evenly with a knife.
  6. Add the pizza to the oven and set a timer for 5-30 minutes depending on hunger levels.
  7. After taking the pizza out of the oven, let it rest. This is not necessary though.
  8. Finally serve the pizza whole with a glass of boot juice.

*(but you'd be wrong)

Serves one poor soul
